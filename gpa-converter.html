How the global GPA conversion works

Every grading system can be mapped to the US 4.0 scale through a shared reference: the US letter grade and its point value. When you pick a country and scale, the calculator knows exactly how each local grade translates.

For every course, the engine runs the three steps admissions offices use:

  • Translate your local grade into its US point equivalent.
  • Weight it by credits: Quality Points = US Points × Credits.
  • Combine: Cumulative GPA = total Quality Points ÷ total Credits.

Everything recalculates instantly, and your form is saved in your browser so a refresh never loses your work.

Which grading systems does this support?

Over 30, including US 4.0, UK Honours, ECTS Europe, India 10-point CGPA and percentage, Pakistan HEC 4.0 and percentage, Canada, Australia, Germany, France, China, Japan, and more.

How are grades converted to a US GPA?

Each local grade is translated to its US point equivalent, multiplied by credits to get quality points, then total quality points are divided by total credits.

Can I convert a US GPA back to my country's scale?

Yes. Switch to US → Local mode, enter a US 4.0 GPA, and see the equivalent in Indian CGPA, ECTS, percentage and other systems.

Is it free with no limits?

Completely free with no daily limit, no account and no paywall. You can export a full PDF report for free too.
