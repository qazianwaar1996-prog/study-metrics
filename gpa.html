How to calculate your GPA

Your GPA is a weighted average of your grades, where each course counts in proportion to its credit hours. A 4-credit course affects your GPA twice as much as a 2-credit one.

  • Convert each letter grade to its grade point value (A = 4.0, B = 3.0, and so on).
  • Multiply each course's grade points by its credit hours to get "quality points."
  • Add up all the quality points, then divide by the total number of credits.

So an A (4.0) in a 3-credit class and a B (3.0) in a 4-credit class gives (4.0 × 3 + 3.0 × 4) ÷ 7 = 3.43 GPA.
