What is CGPA and how is it calculated?

CGPA, or Cumulative Grade Point Average, is the weighted average of your grades across every semester of your program, not just one term.

The calculation is a credit-weighted average of your semester GPAs:

  • Multiply each semester's GPA by the total credits earned that semester to get its "quality points."
  • Add up the quality points from every semester.
  • Divide by the total credits across all semesters.
Where do I find my semester GPA and credits?

They're on your official transcript or student portal, usually listed per term. Enter each semester's GPA and its total credit hours into a row above.

Does my data leave my device?

No. Everything runs in your browser and your numbers stay local. Nothing is uploaded anywhere.
