How the target GPA calculation works

Your cumulative GPA is a credit-weighted average of everything you've taken. To find what you need going forward, we work backwards from your goal.

Using the result

Treat the required average as your semester benchmark. If it says 3.8, you know every remaining term needs to sit near an A- average.

What if the required GPA is above 4.0?

It means your goal can't be reached from your remaining credits alone. Even straight A's wouldn't pull the average high enough.

What counts as "credits completed"?

All credit hours that already have a final grade factored into your GPA.
