How the target GPA calculation works

Your cumulative GPA is a credit-weighted average of everything you've taken. To find what you need going forward, we work backwards from your goal. First we calculate the total quality points your goal GPA requires across all your credits, then subtract the quality points you've already banked. What's left has to come from your remaining credits.

The formula is: required GPA = (goal × total credits − current GPA × completed credits) ÷ remaining credits. If the answer comes out above 4.0, your goal isn't reachable with your remaining courses alone.

Using the result

Treat the required average as your semester benchmark. If it says 3.8, you know every remaining term needs to sit near an A- average. Sliding the goal up and down shows instantly how ambitious targets translate into the effort required.

What if the required GPA is above 4.0?

It means your goal can't be reached from your remaining credits alone, because even straight A's wouldn't pull the cumulative average high enough. Either lower the goal slightly or, if your school allows, look at retaking courses to replace low grades.

What counts as "credits completed"?

All credit hours that already have a final grade and are factored into your current GPA. Don't include courses currently in progress or ones you haven't taken yet.

Is my data saved?

No. The calculation runs entirely in your browser and nothing is uploaded. Your inputs stay on your device.
