How the final grade calculation works

Your final course grade is a weighted blend of two things: the work you've already done and your final exam. If the final is worth 30%, then your existing grade counts for the other 70%. To find the score you need, we solve for the final so the two pieces add up to your goal.

The formula is: required final = (goal − current × (1 − weight)) ÷ weight, with the weight expressed as a decimal.

What if I need more than 100%?

It means your target can't be reached with the final exam alone. Consider whether extra credit is available, or set a slightly lower goal that's actually achievable.

Where do I find my current grade and the final's weight?

Your current grade is usually in your course portal. The final's weight is listed in your syllabus.
